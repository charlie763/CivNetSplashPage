Questions People Ask Us

Have a question that isn't answered below? Email help@civnet.com.

What Platforms are Integrated Through CivNet?

Currently, CivNet enables group members to communicate from email, text, and Slack. We plan to add Facebook Messenger next.

Why Won’t My Message Go Out?

The most common reason a message won’t go out is that the CivNet Bot wants you to complete a task first. For example, when a group is first created, members have to set their communication preferences before they can post. The CivNet Bot will ask them to set their preferences by responding "#text", "#email" or "#both". Users have to complete this task before they can send a message, otherwise we don’t know if you’re trying to talk to your group or the bot.

What Does CivNet Cost?

CivNet is FREE! We plan to add paid premium features in the future, but all current services are and will be free unless otherwise noted.

Are CivNet Groups Private?

Yes, CivNet groups are private. Only you and your members can see your group. In the future, we may add the ability to mark your group as discoverable. Non-members still wouldn’t be able to see group membership or content, but they could search for groups by themes and request to join yours.

How Do I Add Members to a CivNet Group?

You have to be the admin of a group to add members. You can do so by clicking on the “members” tab in your group page on CivNet. People can also request to join your group by sending a message to the group's dedicated phone number or email address.

What Happens When I Add Someone to a Group?

They get a CivNet bot message that says you have added them to your group, and the bot asks them to set their communication preferences.

How do Group Members Set Communication Preferences?

When a group admin adds a member to a group, that person immediately gets a message from a CivNet bot asking them to set their communication preferences. Group members can also edit their preferences by editing their profile on the CivNet web app.

How Big is an Average CivNet Group?

We suggest using CivNet with groups of 5-50 people for internal group communication.

How is Messaging Organized?

All CivNet messages are at the same level of hierarchy. There are no comment level messages or threads. This is done primarily to accommodate text messaging. In the future we plan to implement the ability to search conversation by phrases or tags.

Can I Message Individuals or Subgroups?

Currently, all CivNet messages are “reply all”. We may implement other options in the future. For now, we suggest creating separate groups for subgroups.

Can I Send Documents?

Document sending and receiving is currently only available in-site and through email communication. We plan to provide the ability to send and receive documents via text and Slack in the near future.

What Type of Privacy Does CivNet Have?

CivNet employs standard security protocols for a web app to protect your private information. We do not currently have encryption for messages, but we are considering adding this in the future.

Does CivNet Have Integration For Events and Calendars?

No. But, this has been asked for multiple times, and we are strongly considering adding it in the future.
